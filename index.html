About MBN

Built by the Community, for the Community

Co-founded in 2024 by PK Daigo and Aileen Arnold, the Marianas Business Network was created to fill a critical gap in the CNMI's business ecosystem. Unlike traditional chambers of commerce that charge membership fees and cater to established enterprises, MBN is open to everyone — small business owners, freelancers, photographers, side-hustle entrepreneurs, and anyone looking to grow.

We believe economic empowerment starts at the grassroots level. Through free educational seminars, government dialogues, networking mixers, and our flagship Marianas Business Showcase, MBN has become the most active and innovative business organization in the Marianas.

From Saipan to Guam and beyond, MBN is building the infrastructure for a connected, resilient Pacific Island economy — one relationship at a time.

"I want to be a solution to the island and not somebody who is going to sit back and not take action in regards to our economy and help one another."

— PK Daigo, Co-Founder & President

In Loving Memory

Governor Arnold Indalecio Palacios

August 22, 1955 — July 23, 2025

10th Governor of the Commonwealth of the Northern Mariana Islands

Before MBN had a track record, before anyone knew our name, Governor Arnold I. Palacios believed in what we were building. On January 9, 2025, he walked into our event at the 360 Beach Club in Susupe — not as an obligation, but as a champion. He addressed our members directly, spoke to the heart of grassroots business, and lent the full weight of his office to a movement that many had dismissed.

He chose MBN. He saw in us what we were still learning to see in ourselves: that the small businesses, the side hustles, the freelancers, and the dreamers of the Marianas are the real backbone of this economy. His presence that evening was not a photo opportunity — it was a declaration that the voices of everyday entrepreneurs matter at the highest level of government.

Governor Palacios passed away on July 23, 2025. The CNMI lost a public servant of unwavering integrity, compassion, and quiet strength. But his legacy endures in every MBN event we hold, every small business owner we support, and every bridge we build between community and government.

We carry his belief forward. Every day.

With deepest gratitude and enduring respect,
The Marianas Business Network Family
